Design reflection

Identity and Culture

I think my mostly cool toned color palatte generally represents a laid back and calmer energy, but my favorite part of the palatte is the bright orange color. Orange is my impractical favorite color because it's so vibrant and generally associated with energy and fun in the west which is the vibe I'm going for. As for the typography, I chose 2 fonts that are also more on the calming, buisness side. I think this is good because it will (eventually) put more focus on my work and the color of the page, which I think are better representations of myself.

Accessibility considerations

It was originally hard for me to find a color pallate that would work with white background since I plan on my pages being white/light colored. A lot of the colors that I wanted to use were not WCAG complient with white background. I ended up choosing 2 lighter colors and 3 darker colors to help with maintaining that balance.

Design Process

What suprised me the most was having to think about the margins, borders, and padding. When designing something, I have never really had to think about the number of pixels between the elements. I have always been able to click and drag my design elements on a page. When I start working on styling my portfolio project I want to play around with my text sizes and spacing to make it feel more like me.

Future Applications

I might change any/all of the elements that I decided on today as I start to work on developing my site. It's hard to tell exaclty what will work in different contexts. It's really easy to change things like text size or color when I need to. I don't know if anything feels really set in stone right now, but as I go on I think things like spacing and colors will be harder to change.
